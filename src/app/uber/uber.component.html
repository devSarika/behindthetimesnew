Uber Partners With Flipkart To Deliver Essential Products Amid Coronavirus Lockdown in India 


            

            In a bid to curb the spread of COVID-9 across India, PM Narendra Modi had announced a 21-day country-wide lockdown.
            As a part of the lockdown, several grocery stores and other shops have been shut down. However, several e-commerce 
            platforms have stepped forward to deliver essentials to users. To that end, Uber recently announced that it has tied
            up with Flipkart,to offer its customers access to essential supplies across Delhi, Mumbai and Bangalore.

            so all the money earned would be directly given to drivers. “This marks our third such B2B partnership within a week,
             following those with bigbasket and Spencer’s Retail [...] to facilitate delivery of essential supplies to consumers. 
             All these deals help in keeping citizens indoors so we can collectively support the government in helping contain
            the spread of COVID-19”, mentioned Prabhjeet Singh, Director - Operations, and Head of Cities, Uber India & South Asia,
