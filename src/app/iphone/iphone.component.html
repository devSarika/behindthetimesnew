'iPhone 12 Pro' may add LiDAR sensor to triple rear camera bump' 


            

            We’re far away from the launch of the iPhone 12 series that is said to consist of four different models this
            time. Heck, even the 2020 iPhone SE (also addressed as the iPhone 9) hasn’t been launched yet. But a new
            leak on the internet now gives us a look at the alleged camera setup of the upcoming iPhone 12 which shared
            an important component that was recently introduced with the 2020 iPad Pro lineup.


            The image of the phone was found in a build of iOS 14 by the folks at Concepts iPhone. The image doesn’t
            show the camera module in flash and is not even a render that might give us an idea on what the upcoming
            phone might look like. Instead, the image gives us a skeletal look of the camera module.


            The camera array seems to be that of the iPhone 12 Pro and the iPhone 12 Pro Max (names not confirmed).
            Apart from the wide, ultrawide and the telephoto sensors that we also saw on the iPhone 11 Pro series, there
            is a fourth sensor present too. This is said to be a LIDAR (LIght Detection And Ranging) scanner that made
            its debut with the iPad Pro series that launched last month.
            SEE ALSO: Apple's Upcoming 2020 iPhone SE Could Launch Any Minute Now
            To accommodate the fourth sensor, Apple has seemed to shift the flash to the sensor while the four sensors
            sit around it.


            Before this new image surfaced, there have been rumours of Apple including a depth sensor for its higher-end
            iPhones - initially expected to launch this fall. Given that the LIDAR scanner was recently featured in the
            iPads, there is a high probability that it will also make it’s way to the new iPhones.


            With the launch of ARKit back in 2017, Apple’s iPhones and iPads now make up for the biggest augmented
            reality platform in the world. While we’ve seen a lot of Android smartphone manufacturers implement depth
            sensors for AR-based applications, none have quite been able to match the dexterity of what Apple has to
            offer.


            SEE ALSO: Apple Reveals New iPad Pro with LiDAR Scanner, Trackpad Support, And More
            With the inclusion of a LIDAR scanner on the next-gen iPhones, Apple is only looking too further widen the
            gap. Reviews of the new 2020 Apple iPad Pro have shown how well the new sensor performs albeit have pointed
            out the limited use given the device’s form factor. But the LIDAR on the iPhone 12 Pro series will
            definitely increase the use of a wider range of applications.


            Apple is said to launch up to 4 different models in the iPhone 12 series later this year: a 5.4-inch model,
            two 6.1-inch models, and an all-new 6.7-inch variant. All the new phones are expected to have support for 5G
            networks - a first for Apple - and will come with OLED panels that are reportedly manufactured by Samsung
            and LG.
